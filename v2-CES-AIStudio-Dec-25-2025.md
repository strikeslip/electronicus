### 1. Buchla (100 Series / 200 Series / Music Easel)
2. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buchla_Electronic_Musical_Instruments

*   **History:** Created by **Don Buchla** in 1963 in Berkeley, CA. Commissioned by Morton Subotnick and Ramon Sender, it prioritized "West Coast Synthesis"—focusing on complex oscillators, waveshaping, and low-pass gates rather than traditional filters.
*   **Prominent Artists & Examples:**
    *   **Suzanne Ciani:** "The Eighth Wave" (performed on the Buchla 200). 
        URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F0S_mZ6Bf0k
    *   **Caterina Barbieri:** "Fantas" (utilizing Buchla 200 patterns).
        URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3R4E6zD0e8M
*   **Software Versions:**
    *   Arturia Buchla Easel V: https://www.arturia.com/products/software-instruments/buchla-easel-v/overview
*   **Javascript Examples:**
    *   Buchla-style Low Pass Gate Implementation: https://etcetc.github.io/buchla-lpg-js/
*   **Supercollider/Github:**
    *   Buchla 200 Series SC Implementation: https://github.com/supercollider-quarks/Buchla

### 1. Korg M1 (Bestselling)
2. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korg_M1

*   **History:** Released in 1988, designed by the **Korg Engineering Team**. It pioneered the "Music Workstation" concept by combining a high-fidelity digital synthesizer (AI Synthesis), a sequencer, and digital effects in one unit.

### 1. Yamaha DX7 (Popular 1980’s)
2. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yamaha_DX7

*   **History:** Developed by **Yamaha** based on Frequency Modulation (FM) synthesis research by **John Chowning** at Stanford University. Released in 1983, it was the first commercially successful digital synth.
*   **Prominent Artists & Examples:**
    *   **John Chowning:** "Stria" (The foundation of FM).
        URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9S7EAnSlo9g
    *   **Brian Eno:** "An Ending (Ascent)" (Deeply programmed DX7 textures).
        URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hZ_HdfW4Cps
*   **Software Versions:**
    *   Dexed (Open Source): https://asb2m10.github.io/dexed/
*   **Javascript Examples:**
    *   FM7 Algorithm Logic: https://etcetc.github.io/dx7-fm-js/

### 1. Moog Minimoog
2. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minimoog

*   **History:** Built by **Bill Hemsath and Robert Moog** in 1970. It was the first synthesizer to feature a built-in keyboard, designed for portability and ease of use in live performance.
*   **Prominent Artists & Examples:**

### 1. Sequential Prophet-5
2. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prophet-5

*   **History:** Designed by **Dave Smith and John Bowen** in 1977. It was the first fully programmable polyphonic synthesizer with patch memory, utilizing the SSM (later Curtis) chips.
*   **Prominent Artists & Examples:**
    *   **John Carpenter:** "Halloween Theme" (Analog suspense).
        URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vl09qAR_Zz8
    *   **Boards of Canada:** "Everything You Do is a Balloon" (Prophet textures).
        URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A2zKARkpDW4
